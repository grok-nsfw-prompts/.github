## How to Get Started

Getting started with Prompt CAT is incredibly simple:

### Step 1: Sign Up

Create your [free account](https://promptcat.io/signin) in seconds. We support email sign-up and social login (Google, Facebook) for your convenience.

### Step 2: Claim Your Daily Credits

Once logged in, you'll automatically receive 20 free credits. These refresh every 24 hours, so you can keep creating daily.

### Step 3: Choose Your Model

Navigate to either [Image AI](https://promptcat.io/image) or [Video AI](https://promptcat.io/video) from the header menu. Browse our collection of models and select the one that fits your creative vision.

### Step 4: Write Your Prompt

Describe what you want to create. Be specific about:
- Subject and composition
- Style and mood
- Colors and lighting
- Technical details (resolution, aspect ratio)

Not sure where to start? Check out our [prompt gallery](https://promptcat.io/home) for inspiration and ready-to-use prompts.

### Step 5: Generate and Download

Hit generate and watch the AI bring your vision to life. Once complete, download your creation in high quality or share it directly to social media.

## Tips for Better Results

### Be Specific

Instead of "a cat," try "a fluffy orange tabby cat sitting on a windowsill, golden hour lighting, photorealistic style."

### Use Style References

Mention artistic styles, photography techniques, or famous artists to guide the aesthetic: "in the style of Studio Ghibli" or "cinematic lighting like a Wes Anderson film."

### Experiment with Models

Different models excel at different things. [Grok Imagine](https://promptcat.io/grok-imagine-prompts) is great for bold, artistic images. [Nano Banana Pro](https://promptcat.io/nano-banana-prompts) delivers photorealism. Try multiple models to find your favorite.

### Learn from the Community

Browse our [prompt gallery](https://promptcat.io/home) to see what works. Every creation shows the exact prompt used, so you can learn techniques from successful generations.

### Start Simple, Then Refine

Begin with a basic prompt, see the results, then add more details to refine. This iterative approach helps you understand how each model interprets your instructions.
